One year has passed since my arrival in Germany. This year brought many firsts – my first time living abroad, visiting dentists, exploring European cities, speaking basic German words, and meeting an online friend in person. I’ve settled into a long-term apartment, completed my probation period, and attended a developer conference. The worries I had last year have faded away, and I’ve found a sense of peace in my heart.

Although I didn’t spend much time on open courses this year, I managed to complete the Paxos Lab 3A. I’ve realized how much my system programming skills have grown since I started studying operating systems four years ago. Back then, I would struggle to understand lab assignments even after reading the materials multiple times. Now I can grasp the concepts and implement solutions – it just takes time to turn my ideas into code. I’ve gained confidence to achieve things I once thought impossible. Yet, I sometimes wonder if I’ll be able to overcome the challenges that lie ahead. Will I continue to grow and adapt?

The AI era has begun. The capabilities and potential of AI will inevitably affect everyday life and industries. It’s unpredictable how people will find their place in this rapidly changing world. I experimented with Claude to implement a vacation-planner tool, and it created a version that met my basic requirements. It still has limitations – inconsistent responses, recurring coding errors, and challenges in fully aligning with human intent. Nevertheless, I believe it will evolve into a more intelligent version where everyone can implement their ideas without deep programming knowledge. Still, understanding the fundamental principles remains valuable for building complex projects. Andrej Karpathy wrote a short AI story where “shapers” – a new type of professional – would assist AI agents in solving human-intelligence tasks. It will be fascinating to see how super-intelligent AI ultimately develops.

About the relationship aspect, I finally resolved the emotional issue that had bothered me for years. It was a relief to talk straightforwardly to her about all the feelings I had when we were very young. Going through this helped me discover who I really am. Though it’s still difficult to look back at my old self struggling during those years, I’ve stopped hiding the past and moved on. I told her I would visit Iceland after graduation, and I’m glad I kept my word.

Speaking German was challenging for me, even with simple words. I was hesitant due to my lack of prior knowledge of the language. This changed after meeting with a friend who taught me some basic phrases, like how to say “pay by card,” “pay in cash,” and “thank you.” That’s all I’ve got for now :) I plan to start learning German more seriously and aim to reach the B1 level next year.

In the end, I would like to thank my friends and parents for their companionship, understanding, and support.

Games¶

I bought an Xbox earlier this year, and my main game console has shifted from the Switch to the Xbox. I have to admit that gaming has become somewhat boring for me now, though it remains a good way to relax. I even talked a friend into playing video games.

Baldur’s Gate 3: ✅ Great game. Completed the mainline story.

Persona 3 Reload: Good game, though some female character portrayals made me uncomfortable.

Citizen Sleeper: ✅ Decent game with well-structured gameplay.

Fallout 4: Good game. Strong storytelling, but the constant combat became repetitive.

Assassin’s Creed Odyssey: ✅ Enjoyable overall, apart from the naval battles.

Mount & Blade II: Bannerlord: Decent game. It can explore the map faster than the warband.
